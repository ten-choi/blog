## Vocabulary

- **briefly** (간단히, 잠깐) — used to indicate actions done in a short time, or to explain things concisely.
  - *Could you briefly summarize the report before the meeting starts?*
- **conduct** (n. 행동 / v. 행동하다, 지도하다)
  - *The professor will conduct the experiment in the lab next week.*
- **discrimination** (n. 구별, 차별)
  - *The new law prohibits discrimination in hiring based on age.*
- **acquire** (v. 획득하다)
  - *She acquired a strong command of Japanese after two years in Tokyo.*
- **confidential** (adj. 기밀의)
  - *Please keep these documents confidential and do not share them with anyone outside the team.*
- **evaluation** (n. 평가)
  - *The team will receive a performance evaluation at the end of the quarter.*
- **suspect** (n. 용의자 / adj. 의심스러운)
  - *The police questioned the main suspect for several hours.*
- **factor** (n. 요소, 요인)
  - *Cost was the main factor in our decision.*
- **consent** (n. 동의 / v. 동의하다)
  - *The patient signed a form to give consent for the surgery.*
- **ensure** (v. 안전하게 하다, 지키다)
  - *Please ensure that all the doors are locked before you leave.*
- **adequate** (adj. 충분한)
  - *We don't have adequate funding to finish the project this year.*
- **urgency** (n. 긴급)
  - *The situation called for a sense of urgency from the whole team.*
