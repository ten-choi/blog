# Song Review: ILLIT – It's Me

*Posted on April 30, 2026*

ILLIT has always been pretty good at riding whatever wave K-pop is on right now, which makes their discography super trendy but kind of hit-or-miss. The ILLIT tracks I love most have gone against the grain and carved out something that feels more *them*. With their new single *It's Me*, they're hopping onto the "let's all yap over a techno beat" trend sweeping the industry this year. The result? Fun, but way too hollow.

At its best, *It's Me* has this madcap energy that reminds me of K-pop's more gimmicky early-2010s acts. It's nice seeing a bit of humor work its way back in alongside the pulsing dance beats driving songs like this. *It's Me* is also super referential to K-pop fandom, building its hook around repeated "Who's your bias?" taunts. The catch? The song only has about three ideas, and they get old fast. It's not a long track (2:19, to be exact), but even that runtime feels padded with constant repeats of the hook and the same beat drop. *It's Me* would land harder if it threw in some competing elements — give us a little space between all the shouting and yapping so those big moments actually hit when they come around.

As it stands, *It's Me* is meme music, in the same lane as what fellow HYBE girl groups Katseye and LE SSERAFIM have been putting out lately. That works great for shortform engagement, but it tends to make for a thin discography that doesn't really age well. *It's Me* might be a party, but it's also one more sign that K-pop is drifting away from songs and toward branding exercises and shameless SNS-domination plays.
